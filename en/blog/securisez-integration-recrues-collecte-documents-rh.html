The cost of slow onboarding

Manually chasing IDs, social-security certificates, bank details, and diploma proofs consumes your HR team's schedule. Every missing or expired document triggers another email exchange, with no clear deadline or audit trail. This back-and-forth blocks access to work tools and postpones the new hire's first genuinely productive day. The result: the company pays a salaried worker waiting on administrative clearance, while the file stays incomplete on day one.

Automation secures employee files

An automated workflow activates the moment a contract is signed. The system requests files from the candidate through a secure portal, checks the legibility and validity of each document, and extracts key data straight into your HRIS without re-keying. If a file is illegible, incomplete, or expired, a targeted reminder goes out automatically, with deadline tracking. You guarantee a complete, compliant file before day one, where manual collection lets oversights slip through.

Focus resources on culture

Welcoming talent rests on conveying company culture and supporting the first weeks on the job, not sorting PDF attachments. By relieving your teams of repetitive administrative tasks, automation cuts legal compliance risks and reclaims high-value hours. 4. The automated onboarding workflow, step by step

In practice, a reliable setup chains together a few orchestrated steps that turn a chaotic collection process into a controlled one, from signature to compliant archiving.

  • Trigger and e-signature: as soon as the job offer is signed electronically, the candidate receives a link to a personal portal listing every required document.
  • Upload and automated checks: OCR extraction reads each document, verifies its format, validity date, and data consistency, then flags any missing pieces.
  • Reminders and human validation: automatic reminders target only the outstanding documents, while an HR manager reviews ambiguous cases through human-in-the-loop validation.
  • HRIS integration and GDPR compliance: once the file is complete, the data feeds your HRIS via API, with encrypted storage and retention periods that comply with the GDPR.
